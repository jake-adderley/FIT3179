Data Visualisation II - How well does El Nino/La Nina Predict the weather?

The natural cycle known as the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) is a major influence and climate driver in Australia. With cycles of one to eight years, El Niño and its counterpart La Niña are useful predictors of the general weather behavior in many circumstances. When a high ENSO Index is measured it means the cycle is in El Niño, while a negative index means La Niña.

During El Niño periods there are, on average, fewer tropical cyclones in Australia and a general decrease in rainfall. The opposite is true for La Niña, tending to increase rainfall and tropical storms. While not the only factor that influences storms and rainfall, monitoring ENSO can be a good indicator as can be seen in the charts above and below.
